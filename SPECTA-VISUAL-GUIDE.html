01 — The North Star

The one-sentence DNA

If a frame doesn't pass this sentence, it's off-brand.

“A real object in honest, directional light, framed with confident negative space and a warm cinematic grade — quiet enough to feel premium, sharp enough to stop the scroll.”

What the references have in common

Across 22 saved frames — wallets, watches, caps, jewellery, portraits, even a magazine cover — the same fingerprints repeat:

  • One light, one idea. A single dominant light source and a single subject. Everything else falls to shadow or blur.
  • Hands do the talking. The product is held, pulled, pinched, slipped into a pocket — never just sitting there.
  • Texture in macro. Saffiano weave, stitch, zip teeth, leather grain — shot close enough to feel.
  • Warm + restrained. Earthy backdrops, gold-biased highlights, gentle desaturation, a touch of grain.
  • Big type, used sparingly. When words appear they're huge and few — a headline and a kicker, nothing more.

The feeling to protect

SPECTA's voice is “No logos screaming. No gimmicks.” The imagery has to match: it should look like a considered-design object photographed by someone who knows light — not an ad shouting at you.

Lead with the controlled, art-directed end of the board (studio sets, designed surfaces, sculpted light). Candid “grab-shot” realism reads cheaper and is harder to fake convincingly — use it only when the hand/use-moment is the entire point.

03 — Typography

Type

Outfit does almost everything — geometric, confident, modern. Lora italic is the single editorial grace note (taglines, “Simply Spectacular”). Words are big and few.

CATALOG CAPTION — mono / Outfit 400, small, tracked (the P&Co reference, IMG_4057)
SPECTA — Pop-Up Cardholder · Saffiano · Black / Steel Grey / Deep Navy / Cognac · RFID-safe · €60
The masthead device (from the Numéro cover, IMG_4044): set one word huge and thin, then let the product or hand physically overlap it. It instantly reads “editorial,” not “ad.” Pair a thin oversized word with the bold display line for contrast.

Rules

  • Max two type sizes per frame: a hero line + a kicker.
  • Cream #FEF7EA type on imagery; add a soft dark scrim under it so it never fights the photo.
  • Coral only for a single accent word, the CTA pill, or the colour chip — never a whole sentence.
  • Tight tracking on display (−2 to −3%); open tracking on kickers (+30%).
  • Left- or corner-anchored. Avoid centring everything.

Fonts

Primary Outfit (300–900) · Secondary serif Lora (italic). Both free on Google Fonts. Mono for spec captions only. Keep the brand kit in ~/Downloads/SPECTA/ as the source of truth.

04 — Light & Grade

Light & grade

Six lighting setups cover the whole board. Pick one per frame and commit to it — half-lit beats evenly-lit every time.

Hard key / chiaroscuro

One hard source, deep falloff to black. Dramatic, sculptural. IMG_4046 · 4043

Warm radial spotlight

Glowing amber centre, vignetted edges — the product floats in warmth. IMG_4051

Window-slat rake

Hard daylight through blinds; crisp slat shadows across the surface. IMG_4062 · pedestal-A

Soft daylight

Wrapped north-window light, gentle shadow. The lifestyle/in-use look. IMG_4059 · 4060 · 4061

Dark void

Matte black surround, one soft directional key. Premium flat-lay / hero. IMG_4056

Big soft octabox

Even, clean, top-down wrap for catalog clarity. IMG_4057 · 4058

The grade (apply to every output)

// the warm cinematic look, in words warm white-balance bias (gold highlights, cool-neutral shadows) gentle contrast · slightly lifted blacks · −5% global saturation soft vignette · fine film grain · subtle highlight bloom // matching ffmpeg string used on the SPECTA clips eq=contrast=1.12:saturation=0.95:gamma=0.97, curves=preset=medium_contrast, vignette=PI/4.4, unsharp=5:5:0.45

Notes

  • Black-on-dark is the trap. The black colourway disappears — lift it (gamma +.05, a touch of brightness) and rake light across the saffiano so the weave reads.
  • Keep grain subtle. On video it balloons file size; on stills it sells “photographed, not rendered.”
  • Highlights stay warm; never let them go clinical-blue. Shadows can cool slightly for depth.
  • Always leave a true black point and a true white point — the grade is warm, not muddy.

05 — Composition & Framing

How frames are built

The board is disciplined about space. These are the moves that repeat.

Confident negative space

Subject small or off-centre; let emptiness carry the frame. Type lives in the open area.

IMG_4046 · 4053 · 4057

Hands as the hero

Held, pulled, pinched, slipped away. The grip gives scale, life and “mine.” Add silver rings + a watch for texture.

IMG_4051 · 4059 · 4060 · 4050

Macro texture crop

Fill the frame with saffiano weave, zip teeth, stitch, embossed logo. Shallow DOF, one sharp plane.

IMG_4062 · 4050

Type breaks the subject

Oversized word behind/over the product — the masthead device. Reads editorial, not advert.

IMG_4044 · 4041

Impossible physics

Float, balance, stack against gravity. Playful, premium, scroll-stopping for the family-of-4 shot.

IMG_4058

Designed geometry

Stack the colourways on coloured blocks/pedestals at staggered heights; hard light casts long shadows.

pedestal-A
Formats & safe zones. Shoot/generate 4:5 for feed and 9:16 for story/reel. Keep all type inside the centre ~80% — leave the top ~12% and bottom ~18% clear of words on 9:16 so platform UI never clips the headline or CTA.

07 — Logo Lock

Never let AI touch the logo

The single most important rule. Generators love to “improve” the mark — historically SPECTA → BETA. Lock it, every time.

Always seed from real reference photos / clean cutouts (the studio shots + transparent cut-*.png cutouts) — never from an AI-generated image. Then append this exact paragraph to every prompt:
KEEP THE SPECTA LOGO EXACTLY AS IN THE REFERENCE PHOTOS — same "SPECTA" wordmark on the rail + same embossed hexagonal S logomark; identical lettering / spelling / font / proportions; do NOT redesign, restyle, re-spell, recolour or invent any logo or text; keep it pixel-faithful.

The mark

Hexagonal “S” logomark + “SPECTA” wordmark, embossed/debossed tone-on-tone on the leather and printed on the metal rail. It whispers — keep it subtle, never recoloured to coral on the product itself.

After generating — always check

  • Spelling reads S-P-E-C-T-A at 100% zoom.
  • Hex logomark shape intact (6 sides, centred S).
  • Saffiano cross-hatch weave present, not smoothed plastic.
  • Slider/rail + card-staircase geometry correct.
  • Hands: 5 fingers, believable nails/skin.

08 — Quick Gut-Check

Do / Don't

✓ Do

  • One dominant light, one subject, generous negative space.
  • Warm cinematic grade + a touch of grain on every output.
  • Real hands, real surfaces, real saffiano texture in macro.
  • Lead with controlled studio / designed sets (pedestal, float, reflective).
  • Two type sizes max; cream type on a soft scrim.
  • Seed from real photos; lock the logo; check spelling after.

✕ Don't

  • Flat, even, shadowless studio light — it kills the mood.
  • Clinical blue / over-saturated HDR grades.
  • Cheap candid “grab shots” unless the gesture is the whole point.
  • Cluttered frames or competing focal points.
  • Walls of copy, centred everything, coral sentences.
  • Luxury / heritage / hustle clichés, or screaming logos.

09 — Generation Cheat-Sheet

The base prompt skeleton

Tuned for nano_banana_pro (4:5 / 9:16, 2k) seeded with real refs. Swap the bracketed parts; the tail stays constant.

// 1 · SUBJECT + FINISH [shot archetype] of the SPECTA pop-up cardholder in [black / steel grey / deep navy / cognac] saffiano leather, // 2 · COMPOSITION [in two hands / on a pedestal set / floating stack / pulled from pocket / flat-lay], confident negative space, // 3 · SET + SURFACE on [greige seamless / polished black glass / pale studio-blue / tan canvas / coloured pedestals], // 4 · LIGHT [single hard key / warm amber radial / window-slat rake / soft north daylight / dark void + soft key], // 5 · CAMERA + GRADE shot on [50mm f/2 / 85mm f/2.8 / 35mm f/5.6], shallow depth of field, warm cinematic grade, gentle contrast, slightly desaturated, soft vignette, fine film grain, ultra-realistic product AND scene, photographic — not rendered, aspect [4:5 / 9:16], 2k. // 6 · LOGO-LOCK (never edit) KEEP THE SPECTA LOGO EXACTLY AS IN THE REFERENCE PHOTOS — same "SPECTA" wordmark on the rail + same embossed hexagonal S logomark; identical lettering/spelling/font/proportions; do NOT redesign, restyle, re-spell, recolour or invent any logo or text; keep it pixel-faithful.

Seed references to attach

  • Clean cutout (cut-[colour].png) — best for a crisp logo.
  • Real closed-front photo of that colourway.
  • Real side-rail photo (locks the “SPECTA” wordmark).
  • A real cards-popped frame (mechanism accuracy).
  • One style ref from inspo-2/ for mood.
